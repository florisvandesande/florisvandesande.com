Apple Cider Glazed Pork Chops

★★★★

Hoofdgerecht

Kooktijd: 20 mins Moeilijkheidsgraad: Medium Porties: 6 Bron: Food Wishes

Ingrediënten

Ingredients for 6 chops:

1 tbsp vegetable oil

1 tbsp butter

6 boneless center-cut pork chops (6-8 oz each)

salt and pepper to taste

3 cloves minced garlic

1/4 cup apple cider vinegar

2 cups apple cider

1 tsp Dijon mustard

1 tsp minced rosemary

pinch of red chili flakes

Bereidingswijze

1. Season the pork chops generously on both sides with some salt and pepper.

2. In a heavy skillet over medium-high heat, add the vegetable oil and butter. As soon as the butter turns a sort of nutty brown color, add the pork chops to the skillet and fry them for about 5 to 7 minutes on each side. (Don't be afraid to turn the heat down to medium during this process if needed; the stuff at the bottom of the skillet should start to caramelize, but NOT turn black.)

3. After the chops have been cooked on both sides, remove them onto a plate and turn off the heat. Add the garlic to the empty skillet and stir it around with a wooden spoon for about 20 to 30 seconds. We don't want the garlic to brown much, just start sizzling a little bit.

4. Once the garlic is sizzling, add the apple cider vinegar (which stops the garlic from browning and deglazes the pan). After stirring the garlic and vinegar a bit, add the actual apple cider and the Dijon mustard and mix everything well. Crank the heat up here, because we're looking for the mixture to boil and reduce down to a glaze. It won't take long at all, so don't walk away!

5. After the glaze has reduced a bit, turn the heat back down to medium, then stir in the rosemary and cayenne pepper (or red chili flakes, if you have some). You may also taste the mixture at this point to see if it needs more salt and pepper.

6. Once it has reduced a little more, add your pork chops back to the pan. One or two minutes on each side should suffice here.

7. Remove the pork chops again, and you're ready to serve! Feel free to spoon some more of the apple cider sauce onto your pork chops if that's your thing.
